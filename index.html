About Me

Hello, I am Gabriel Waite, currently a PhD student at the University of Technology Sydney, studying Quantum Complexity and Algorithms under the supervision of Michael Bremner and Troy Lee. I am studying with a Sydney Quantum Academy Scholarship starting in April 2023, running until 2027. My main interests lie with Local Hamiltonian Complexity Theory, Ground State Preparation and Quantum Optimisation Algorithms. As a part of my research I created the Hamiltonian Jungle — an online repository for classifying known Hamiltonian Complexity problems! I have also undertaken some courses as per the Sydney Quantum Academy Scholarship; for some of these courses I have GitHub repositories. One of my larger contributions was towards a project on Hamiltonian Simulation via Quantum Signal Processing. To accompy this piece of work, I wrote a small essay summarising the ideas.

My background is in Theoretical Physics. I graduated from the University of Leeds in 2022 with a First Class Honours degrees, MPhys and BSc in Theoretical Physics. I specialised in Quantum Optics, focusing on using a new local position space field theory construction for photons. My Master's thesis studied locally acting mirror Hamiltonians using said theory.

During my undergraduate degree I enjoyed many aspects of Theoretical Physics. My main interests lie with:

  • Quantum Field Theory
  • General Relativity
  • Quantum Information Science
  • Symmetries and Particle Physics
